My Sanctuary & My Safe Place

To Aliyah,

It has taken me six long years to fully realize that when I left, I didn't just walk away from a relationship—I walked away from my own heart. Finding our way back to each other after all this time has been the greatest gift I could ever ask for, but I carry the weight of those five months of silence we recently shared. I know I was the one who hesitated. I was scared that too much time had passed, and I let that fear stop me from telling you how I truly felt until it almost felt too late. I even tried to manipulate the circumstances because I was terrified of losing you again and didn't know how to just be honest.

Now that we have reconnected, I want to spend every single day making things right. I want to be your safe place.

I know life hasn't been easy for you. I know you've carried burdens and walked through traumatic events that you should never have had to face alone.

I want to be that "someone" you can lean on, the person you seek refuge in when the whole world feels like it's trying to knock you down. When everything feels like it’s falling apart around you, I want my arms to be the constant that keeps you grounded and safe.

This time, my love for you is unconditional and wholehearted. I don’t want to control you or your journey; I want to be the one standing right behind you, supporting every step you take toward your dreams. I want to see you fly higher than you ever thought possible, and I want to be the place you come home to when you're tired.

I am here for the long term, Aliyah. I’m not going anywhere this time. You are my Lily—elegant and resilient—and my Baby’s Breath—delicate and pure. I promise to cherish every part of you, forever.

WITH ALL MY LOVE,

Always Yours
